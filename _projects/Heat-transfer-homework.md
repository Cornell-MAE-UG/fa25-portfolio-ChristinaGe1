For this portfolio assignment, I selected Problem Set 2 from MAE 3240. This assignment was valuable to me because it helped me connect heat transfer equations to real physical situations instead of just treating them as abstract formulas. In Problem 1, I analyzed heat loss from a water heater with and without insulation, which showed clearly how adding thermal resistance reduces heat loss and lowers the required power input. In Problem 2, I worked through conduction and convection together for a thin film heater problem, which helped me better understand how thermal resistances in series determine temperature rise and allowable heat flux. These problems made me more comfortable setting up assumptions, identifying the correct heat transfer mode, and choosing the right governing equations.

This assignment will be useful to me because it strengthened the way I approach engineering thermal problems step by step. I learned how to translate a physical system into a simplified thermal model, apply boundary conditions, and interpret the final result in an engineering context. That is important not only for heat transfer coursework, but also for real design problems involving insulation, electronics cooling, and material selection. Overall, this homework improved both my technical understanding and my confidence in organizing a complete engineering solution.
